Our Research

Functional Optimization of Ingredients and Processes

Our research focuses on optimizing dairy processing methods to enhance bioactive protein preservation and functionality. We investigate various processing parameters and their effects on protein structure and bioactivity.

  • Heat treatment optimization
  • Spray drying process development
  • Storage condition effects
  • Novel processing technologies

Milk Bioactive Peptides

We investigate the identification and characterization of bioactive peptides in milk and dairy products, with a focus on their functional properties and potential health benefits.

  • Novel peptide discovery
  • Functional characterization
  • Structure-activity relationships
  • Bioactivity mechanisms

Protein Digestion and Absorption

Our research examines the digestive fate of milk proteins and peptides, from initial digestion to absorption and potential systemic effects.

  • In vitro digestion modeling
  • Absorption kinetics
  • Bioavailability studies
  • Metabolic effects

Bitter Peptides in Dairy Products

We study the formation and impact of bitter peptides in dairy products, developing strategies to minimize bitterness while maintaining nutritional value.

  • Bitter peptide identification
  • Formation mechanisms
  • Mitigation strategies
  • Sensory impact assessment

Infant Nutrition and Digestion

Our research explores the unique aspects of infant digestion and nutrition, with a focus on milk bioactivity and its role in infant development.

  • Infant digestion modeling
  • Preterm infant nutrition
  • Formula development
  • Growth and development
